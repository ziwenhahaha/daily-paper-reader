# GenerativeMPC: VLM-RAG-guided Whole-Body MPC with Virtual Impedance for Bimanual Mobile Manipulation 

Marcelino Julio Fernando, Miguel Altamirano Cabrera, Jeffrin Sam, Yara Mahmoud, Konstantin Gubernatorov, and Dzmitry Tsetserukou 

Abstract — Bimanual mobile manipulation requires a seam-less integration between high-level semantic reasoning and safe, compliant physical interaction—a challenge that end-to-end models approach opaquely and classical controllers lack the context to address. This paper presents GenerativeMPC, a hierarchical cyber-physical framework that explicitly bridges semantic scene understanding with physical control parame-ters for bimanual mobile manipulators. The system utilizes a Vision-Language Model with Retrieval-Augmented Generation (VLM-RAG) to translate visual and linguistic context into grounded control constraints, specifically outputting dynamic velocity limits and safety margins for a Whole-Body Model Predictive Controller (MPC). Simultaneously, the VLM-RAG module modulates virtual stiffness and damping gains for a uni-fied impedance-admittance controller, enabling context-aware compliance during human-robot interaction. Our framework leverages an experience-driven vector database to ensure con-sistent parameter grounding without retraining. Experimental results in MuJoCo, IsaacSim, and on a physical bimanual platform confirm a 60% speed reduction near humans and safe, socially-aware navigation and manipulation through semantic-to-physical parameter grounding. This work advances the field of human-centric cybernetics by grounding large-scale cognitive models into predictable, high-frequency physical control loops. 

I. I NTRODUCTION 

Autonomous robots operating in human-centered environ-ments must navigate unstructured spaces, manipulate objects with varying compliance requirements, and interpret instruc-tions from non-expert users. Bimanual mobile manipulators, platforms combining a mobile base with two articulated arms, are uniquely positioned to address these demands. However, their complexity exposes a fundamental tension: the semantic richness required for task understanding and the physical precision required for safe interaction have been handled by separate, incompatible systems. This fragmen-tation prevents the realization of a unified cybernetic loop where high-level cognitive reasoning directly modulates the robot’s physical reflexes. Recent advances in Vision-Language Models (VLMs) have demonstrated remarkable zero-shot reasoning over vi-sual scenes [1], [2]. However, existing VLM-based planners typically output discrete subgoals, leaving physical compli-ance behavior, how stiff or yielding the robot should be during contact, entirely undefined at the semantic level [3],

Separately, whole-body Model Predictive Control (MPC) frameworks enable real-time trajectory optimization for high degree-of-freedom platforms [7], [8]. While these methods have achieved impressive results in collision-free locomotion and manipulation [9], or combined RL-based planning with low-level MPC [10], the velocity constraints and safety margins that shape their behavior are typically fixed at design time. The present work addresses the open problem of ground-ing semantic scene understanding into predictable physical control for bimanual mobile manipulation. Building upon the semantic-to-compliance paradigm established in SafeHu-manoid [11], we extend this logic to a unified cyber-physical framework where whole-body coordination, non-holonomic navigation, and dual-arm compliance are handled jointly. Furthermore, we address the ”memory gap” in robotics; while Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) has trans-formed natural language processing by grounding outputs in factual context [12], its application to experience-driven control loops remains largely unexplored. Section V reports experimental results. Section VI concludes the paper. II. R ELATED WORK 

A. Vision-Language Models for Robot Planning 

VLMs have emerged as powerful semantic front-ends for robotic systems. HYPERmotion [1] uses a VLM to select robot morphology and action primitives, coupling this to a motion library and MPC for physically consistent execution. VoxAct-B [14] leverages VLMs to assign roles to each arm in bimanual tasks based on language goals. RoboMIND 2.0 [2] provides a multimodal bimanual dataset of over 310,000 dual-arm trajectories, training a hierarchical VLM planner for grounded subgoal decomposition. MoManipVLA [5] and FALCON [6] further couple foundation models with whole-body MPC for loco-manipulation. Despite this progress, none of these systems use the VLM to generate compliance parameters — stiffness, damping, or velocity constraints — that directly shape physical interaction behavior. 

B. Whole-Body MPC for Mobile Manipulation 

Whole-body MPC has enabled agile movements on high-DoF platforms, ranging from quadrupedal loco-manipulation [7], [9] to task-priority transitions in mobile robots [8]. However, in these frameworks, the safety margins ( dsafe )and velocity envelopes are static. Our work introduces a dynamic constraint-scaling mechanism where the human proximity detected by the VLM and directly dictates the MPC constraints. 

C. Impedance and Compliant Interaction 

Since Hogan’s foundational work [15], virtual spring-damper formulations have underpinned compliant interac-tion. Recent efforts have optimized impedance for precision-compliance tradeoffs [3] and dual-arm coordination [4]. DA-VIL [16] combined reinforcement learning with vari-able impedance for dual-arm coordination. While SafeHu-manoid [11] validated VLM-driven impedance scheduling for tabletop tasks, it was limited to single-arm systems. GenerativeMPC advances the state-of-the-art by unifying the impedance-admittance formulation across the entire mobile platform—base and arms—allowing for a singular, math-ematically consistent approach to safe, context-aware co-habitation. III. SYSTEM OVERVIEW 

A. Hardware Platform 

The platform consists of a 22 kg differential-drive base (wheel radius r = 0 .085 m, and wheelbase L = 0 .455 m) with two 6-DoF manipulators actuated by Feetech STS3215 servos [17] on two independent serial buses (Fig. 4). A Velodyne VLP-16 LiDAR and a RealSense D435 RGB-D camera provide obstacle point clouds and visual input to the VLM layer, respectively (Fig. 5). All computation runs onboard an NVIDIA Jetson Orin Nano (16 GB) under ROS 2 Humble. 

B. Three-Layer Architecture 

GenerativeMPC organizes control into three hierarchical layers illustrated in Fig. 3: VLM-RAG for semantic parame-ter generation, Whole-Body MPC for trajectory optimization at 10 Hz over N = 7 steps, and Impedance-Admittance for compliant tracking at 50 Hz. Completed episodes are stored in ChromaDB and retrieved to inform future VLM queries. 

C. Simulation and Sim-to-Real 

Robot models were designed in SolidWorks and exported as URDF (Unified Robot Description Format), from which MuJoCo MJCF (MuJoCo XML model format) and IsaacSim USD (Universal Scene Description) files were derived, pre-serving link geometry, inertial properties, and joint limits. A MuJoCo warehouse scene was used for controller de-velopment and an IsaacSim environment for photorealistic sensor validation. The unified simulation loop executes MPC, Fig. 3. V. EXPERIMENTAL RESULTS 

A. Simulation Setup 

Experiments are conducted in MuJoCo and IsaacSim en-vironments. The MuJoCo warehouse scene features shelving units, cylindrical obstacles, and human-shaped mannequins, as shown in Fig. 6. IsaacSim provides photorealistic val-idation of the manipulation pipeline, as shown in Fig. 2. The VLM-RAG module is seeded with five demonstration episodes covering free navigation, human-proximate naviga-tion, and bimanual reaching. 

B. Task 1: Human-Aware Navigation 

The robot navigates to (3 .0, 2.0, 0◦) with two human mannequins along the path. Upon detecting human proxim-ity, the VLM-RAG module retrieves a conservative profile, reducing base velocity from vmax = 0 .25 to 0.10 m/s — a 60 % speed reduction — and raising damping from Di =15 .0 to 25.0, without any manual gain scheduling. The MPC converges to the goal with a position error of 7.8 mm and heading error of 1.4◦ at t = 71 .7 s, as shown in Figs. 1

C. Task 2: Human Hand Avoidance 

Following base convergence, the robot executes a biman-ual reaching task. Upon detection of a human hand entering the workspace, the VLM-RAG module retrieves a compliant profile ( Ki = 8 .0, Di = 25 .0, dsafe = 0 .15 m), slowing arm motion and increasing compliance. A minimum end-effector distance of 0.15 m from the hand is maintained throughout. When the hand departs, nominal parameters are restored automatically. End-effector errors remain below 2 mm after convergence, with no singularity events observed across all tested configurations.

CONCLUSION This paper introduced GenerativeMPC, a hierarchical cyber-physical framework that bridges semantic scene un-derstanding with compliant physical control for bimanual mobile manipulation. A VLM-RAG module derives task-appropriate velocity constraints and safety margins for the whole-body MPC, and stiffness and damping gains for the impedance-admittance controller, directly from cam-era imagery and natural language — without manual gain scheduling or controller retraining. The MPC plans kine-matically consistent trajectories with embedded APF ob-stacle avoidance, while the unified admittance framework applies a consistent spring-damper formulation to both arm compliance and base tracking. Experimental results confirm base navigation to 7.8 mm in simulation and 17.5 mm on hardware, a 60 % speed reduction near humans, and biman-ual end-effector convergence below 2 mm. The sim-to-real gap is fully attributable to Modbus communication latency rather than controller design, as evidenced by the systematic gain reduction in Table I. Future work will focus on full bimanual manipulation validation on hardware, expansion of the ChromaDB episode database across more diverse task scenarios, and reduction of VLM inference latency toward faster semantic-to-physical parameter adaptation.
