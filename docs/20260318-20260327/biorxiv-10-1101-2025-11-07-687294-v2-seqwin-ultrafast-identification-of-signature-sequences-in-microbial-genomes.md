## 摘要
动机：聚合酶链式反应（PCR）能够实现快速且具有成本效益的诊断，但需要预先鉴定出能够对目标微生物群体进行灵敏且特异性检测的基因组区域，本文将其称为微生物特征序列。目前单个物种已有数万个微生物基因组可用，这限制了现有手动和自动特征鉴定方法的应用。能够利用所有可用微生物基因组的现代方法将确保灵敏且准确的 DNA 特征鉴定，并为临床、环境和公共卫生应用提供稳健的病原体检测。结果：Seqwin 构建了加权泛基因组 minimizer 图，并使用遍历算法来鉴定在目标基因组中频繁出现但在非目标基因组中罕见的特征序列。与依赖序列严格存在或缺失的早期工具不同，Seqwin 能够容纳自然的序列变异，并可扩展至极大规模的基因组集合。当应用于艰难梭菌（C. difficile）、结核分枝杆菌（M. tuberculosis）和沙门氏菌（S. enterica）的基因组时，Seqwin 以更低的计算负担回收了比其他方法更多的高质量特征。Seqwin 对近 15,000 个沙门氏菌基因组的分析在 5 分钟内产生了 200 多个候选特征。Seqwin 为长期以来对可扩展微生物特征发现和诊断分析设计的需求提供了一个开源解决方案。可用性：Seqwin 可免费用于学术用途（https://github.com/treangenlab/Seqwin），并可通过 Bioconda 安装。基准测试数据集、输出和脚本可在 Zenodo（https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19176444）上获得。

## Abstract
Motivation: Polymerase chain reaction (PCR) enables rapid, cost-effective diagnostics but requires prior identification of genomic regions that allow sensitive and specific detection of target microbial groups, herein referred to as microbial signature sequences. We introduce Seqwin, an open-source framework designed to automate microbial genome signature discovery. Tens of thousands of microbial genomes are now available for a single species, limiting the application of existing manual and automated approaches for identifying signatures. Modern approaches that are capable of leveraging all available microbial genomes will ensure sensitive and accurate DNA signature identification and enable robust pathogen detection for clinical, environmental, and public health applications. Results: Seqwin builds weighted pan-genome minimizer graphs and uses a traversal algorithm to identify signature sequences that occur frequently in target genomes but remain rare in non-targets. Unlike earlier tools that depend on strict presence or absence of sequences, Seqwin accommodates natural sequence variation and scales to very large genome collections. When applied to genomes from C. difficile, M. tuberculosis, and S. enterica, Seqwin recovered more high-quality signatures than alternative methods with lower computational burden. Seqwin's analysis of nearly 15,000 S. enterica genomes yielded over 200 candidate signatures in 5 minutes. Seqwin provides an open-source solution for the long-standing need for scalable microbial signature discovery and diagnostic assay design. Availability: Seqwin is freely available for academic use (https://github.com/treangenlab/Seqwin) and can be installed via Bioconda. Benchmarking datasets, outputs, and scripts are available on Zenodo (https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19176444).
