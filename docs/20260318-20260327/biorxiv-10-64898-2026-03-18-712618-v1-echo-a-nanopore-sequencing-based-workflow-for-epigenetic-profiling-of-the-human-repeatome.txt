## Abstract 13 

Summary : The human  genome is dominated by repetitive DNA, whose genetic and 14 

epigenetic variation plays a  key  role in  gene regulation, genome stability , and disease .15 

Recent  advances  in  long -read sequencing  now  enable  large -scale , haplotype -resolved ,16 

and DNA methylation -informative  analysis of  the human genome, including  on  previously 17 

inaccessible  complex and  repetitive  regions . However,  the  comprehensive , simultaneous 18 

characterisation of the  “human  repeatome ” remains challenging, largely due to the lack 19 

of comprehensive  tools  integrated in a single  pipeline that  can  capture the full spectrum 20 

of variation across diverse types  of DNA  repeats . Here , we present ECHO, a user -friendly ,21 

Snakemake -based pipeline for the  “(Epi)genomic Characterisation of Human Repetitive 22 

Elements using Oxford Nanopore Sequencing ”. ECHO  provides a reproducible and 23 

scalable  framework  for  end -to -end  analysis of  whole -genome  nanopore  sequencing 24 

data , enabling  integrative  but also tailored  (epi)genetic  analys es of  the human repeatome .25 

Availability and implementation : ECHO is  freely  available at Github: 26 

https://github.com/leenput/ECHO -pipeline , with  the  archived  version at Zenodo: 27 

https://zenodo.org/records/19068468 28

## 1 Introduction 30 

Repetitive DNA elements  account  for more than half of the human genome  (Nurk  et al. ,31 

2022) . These  include  tandem repeats (TRs)  that are  characterized by  arrays of  repeated 32 

DNA  sequence  units , and  interspersed repeats or  transposable elements (TEs ) that  are 33 

scattered  throughout the genome  (Liao  et al. , 2023) . Despite their abundance, repeats 34 

have  remained  underexplored in  human genomics , primarily  due to  technical limitations 35 

of  short -read sequencing and array -based  technologies,  which  are poorly suited to 36 

resolve long, complex , and repetitive regions  (Treangen and Salzberg, 2012) . This 37 

technical barrier, among other  factors, has  contributed to the longstanding 38 

misconception  that  repetitive DNA  lack s function al relevance  (Ohno, 1972) .39 

In recent years, however, t his  perspective  has shifted , since  growing evidence  continues 40 

to  highlight  key  role s of  repetitive DNA  in genome  organization , gene regulation,  evolution ,41 

and disease  (Wright and Todd, 2023; Lawson  et al. , 2023; Fueyo  et al. , 2022) . Specifically, 42 

TR expansions  can  caus e neurological and developmental disorders  (Depienne and 43 

Mandel, 2021) , while  TE -mediated genomic rearrangements and insertional mutagenesis 44 

can  contribute to tumorigenesis and other complex diseases  (Chuong  et al. , 2017) .45 

Beyond sequence -driven  effects , repeats  are tightly regulated by  epigenetic 46 

mechanisms, particularly DNA methylation, to  modulate  transcription and preserve 47 

genome integrity. Importantly,  both  TRs and TEs display  dynamic , context -specific DNA 48 

methylation patterns, which  may  vary across  haplotypes,  cell types, developmental 49 

stages,  environmental cues , and  disease states  (Pappalardo and Barra, 2021; Barbé and 50 

Finkbeiner, 2022) . As such,  to fully  understand  the  regulatory  and  functional  scope of 51 

repeats  at a genome -scale, we  require integrated analysis of their  (epi) gen omic  features. 52 

More recently, t he renewed momentum in repe titive DNA  research has been  catalysed  by 53 

the  advent  of  long -read sequencing  (LRS)  technologies , such as those developed by 54 

Oxford Nanopore Technologies (ONT) and Pacific Biosciences (PacBio)  (Logsdon  et al. ,55 

2020) . These platforms uniquely  enable  genome -wide access to complex  and  repetitive 56 

regions , along with direct measurement of  DNA  methylation , at single -molecule 57 

resolution  and high -accuracy  (Fu  et al. , 2025) . However,  current  LRS -based  repeat 58 

genotyping  tools  are typically  confined  to  specific repeat types  (TR s or TE s, or their 59

Bilgrav Saether and Eisfeldt, 2024; Chu  et al. , 2021) . While  most of  these tools primarily 61 

focus on sequence variation, notable exceptions have demonstrated the potential of LRS 62 

to jointly profile  the  sequence  and methylation  of repeats . For example,  TRGT  enables TR 63

Snakemake -based  pipeline  for  the  “(Epi)genomic  Characterisation of  Human Repetitive 71 

Elements using  Oxford Nanopore Sequencing ”.72 

## 2 Pipeline d escription 73 

2.1  Overview and i mplementation 74 

The  ECHO  pipeline integrates benchmarked bioinformatics tools and custom 75 

Bash /Python  scripts  in an end -to -end  workflow comprising  two  core  stages : (I) ONT data 76 

preprocessing and  phasing, and (II)  repeatome  profiling  across  TR and TE  classes, 77 

integrating both sequence and DNA methylation variation  (Figure 1 A).  The first  phase 78 

handles  raw ONT files , performs quality control  (QC ) and  optional  read filtering , followed 79 

by  read  alignment, variant calling, and haplotype phasing.  The second  phase  leverages 80 

phased  alignment and variant files  to perform  repeat  (epi) genotyping  of  predefined  loci of 81 

interest  at a genome -wide or targeted manner . It  quantifies  haplotype -specific  DNA 82 

methylation  with  single -CpG  and  region -level  resolution ; the latter  aggregated across all 83 

CpGs within  a repeat and its  flanking regions.  Overall, t he pipeline is  implemented  in  a84 

Snakemake  workflow management system  (Köster  et al. , 2021) . All software 85 

dependencies  are  managed  via  Singularity  to ensure reproducibility and portability 86 

(Kurtzer  et al. , 2017) . ECHO  is designed  to run  both  on  unix -based high -performance 87 

computing (HPC) systems  and  on  local  servers .88

2.2  Input  data 110 

As input  ECHO  accepts  ONT sequencing data  in multiple formats, including raw  POD5 111 

files,  or  basecalled  unaligned BAM ( UBAM ), FASTQ , and  BAM  files,  provided that 112 

methylation -aware ONT models have been used for basecalling . In addition,  ECHO 113 

requires repeat  catalogues  of  TR and TE  target regions , defined relative  to the human 114 

reference genome in use (GRCh38/T2T -CHM13v2) . To facilitate standardized analyses, 115 

ECHO  provides  several  publicly available  repeat catalogues  and derived subsets thereof 116 

that are reformatted  for downstream compatibility , including both genome -wide and 117 

targeted panels  (See Github documentation) . Users may also supply custom catalogues 118 

in BED format,  if preferred .119 

2.3  Preprocessing  and phasing 120 

When raw  sequencing  files ( POD5 ) are provided and GPU resources are available, 121 

basecalling  is  performed using  Dorado  (v 1.1.1) in methylation -aware super accuracy 122 

(SUP)  mode . For  UBAM  input, reads are converted to  FASTQ  files  using samtools  (v1.2 2)123 

with  transfer  of methylation tags  (Li  et al. , 2009) . Optional  read filtering is carried out using 124 

Chopper  (v0.11.0) , based on user -defined threshold s for minimum read length and 125 

quality  (De Coster and Rademakers, 2023) . The reads are  then  aligned  to the human 126 

reference genome  (GRCh38  (default ) or T2T -CHM13v2)  using minimap2  (v2. 30 ) with 127 

default settings  for ONT data ( -ax map -ont) . QC metrics for  raw, filtered , and aligned read s128 

are  obtained  using  Cramino  (v1.1.0)  and  Nano Plot  (v1.46 .1 ) (De Coster and Rademakers, 129 

2023) .130 

After alignment,  reads are  processed  for genome -wide variant calling.  Si ngle  nucleotide 131 

variants (SNVs) and  insertions -deletions ( INDELs ) are  identified  using Clair3 (v1. 2.0) 132 

(Zheng  et al. , 2022) , while structural variants  (SVs)  are  detected  with Sniffles2 (v2. 6.3 )133 

(Smolka  et al. , 2024) . LongPhase  (v1.7.3)  is subsequently used for  co -phasing  of small 134 

variants (SNVs and INDELS)  and  SV s in conjunction with  methylation information , after 135 

which reads are haplotagged to produce a phased alignment file  (Lin  et al. , 2022) . Phasing 136 

QC  is conducted using  cramino ( --phased , --karyotype ) (v1.1.0) , which provides  relative 137 

chrX and chrY coverage metrics that are used for sex inference.  The resulting phased  BAM 138 

and  VCF files ar e used  for  downstream repeatome analysis. 139

2.4  Repeatome  profiling 140 

2.4.1  Genome -wide CpG methylation 141 

As a first step, genome -wide CpG methylation is profiled using modkit  (--cpg  --ignore h )142 

(https://github.com/nanoporetech/modkit ) (v0.5.0) , generating both unphased and 143 

phased  methylation pileups from the  phased  BAM file . The resulting bedMethyl files can 144 

be used to extract methylation levels across any region in the reference genome, including 145 

for  the  repeats  of interest  (see 2. 4.2 and 2. 4.3 ). 146 

2.4.2  Tandem repeat s147 

TR genotyping is carried out  with  LongTR  (v 1.2 ) using recommended ONT -optimized 148 

alignment parameters , which has demonstrated high  genotyping  accuracy  and 149 

robustness  in  a recent benchmarking stud y (Ziaei Jam  et al. , 2024; Aliyev  et al. , 2026) .150 

LongTR  takes as input the phased BAM file along with a user -defined TR catalog ue 151 

specifying the target regions  and  generates a  phased VCF output , which is  subsequently 152 

processed  to  integrate  TR genotype s with methylation information . For each TR locus, 153 

overlapping reads are extracted in a haplotype -aware manner and locally realigned to 154 

their respective TR allele sequences and flanking regions. Based on these local 155 

alignments, single -CpG and average methylation levels per TR and their flanking regions 156 

are quantified for each haplotype using modkit  (--cpg  --ignore h  --mod -threshold m:0.8) .157 

In addition, the uTR tool (v1.0.0) is used to decompose the TR motif composition of each 158 

TR allele  (Masutani  et al. , 2023) . The script outputs an annotated VCF file and summary 159 

table reporting the TR allele lengths, sequences, motif structures,  and  methylation at 160 

three  resolution  levels:  single -CpG  within the TR , and regional  averages  across the TR and 161 

its  flanking regions. 162 

By default,  ECHO  uses a genome -wide TR  catalog ue  adapted for compatibility with 163 

LongTR  (1,7 84 ,80 4 loci, GRCh38 ), including short tandem repeats (STRs, 1 -6 bp repeat 164 

units) and variable number tandem repeats (VNTRs,  longer  repeat units)  (English, 2024) .165 

In the  default  pipeline  configuration, downstream TR methylation analysis focuses on  loci 166 

whose canonical  repeat motif  corresponds to an STR and  contains a t least one  CpG site 167 

within the repeat motif  (8,424 loci)  to avoid computational burden . Nevertheless,  ECHO 168 

supports  several alternative, pre -configured TR catalogue s tailored to specific 169

2.4.3  Transposable elements 172 

TE analysis is  separated  in to  two components : 1)  characteri sation of TEs annotated in the 173 

reference genome  (ref -TEs)  and  2)  identification and  characterisation  of TE  insertions  not 174 

present in the reference genome (non -ref -TEs) . For the ref -TEs,  the  workflow  provides 175 

catalog ue s derived from  UCSC Genome Browser  RepeatMasker  tracks  (Casper  et al. ,176 

2026) . By default, a complete GRCh38 TE catalogue is used, with optional support for a 177 

T2T -CHM13v2 catalogue and  TE  type -specific  sub sets  (e.g. LINEs, SINEs, SVAs, helitron, 178 

DNA transposons) . For each  locus in the catalog ue , SNVs and SVs  from phased VCF files 179 

passing all variant filters (FILTER=PASS ) are intersected with the  ref -TE regions using 180 

BCFtools  (Danecek  et al. , 2021)  and bedtools  (Quinlan and Hall, 2010)  to quantify 181 

sequence variation per element.  In parallel, single -CpG and regional methylation levels 182 

are calculated separately for each TE across its body and up stream  and downstream 183 

flanking regions using phased and unphased modkit pileups, yielding allele -specific and 184 

aggregated methylation estimates, respectively.  In the final output  directory , a summary 185 

file  reports  each  ref -TE with its  genomic  location, classification,  regional  methylation 186 

(phased/unphased) , and counts  of overlapping SNV and SVs.  CpG resolutions 187 

methylation values  and overlapping variants  for  each  ref -TE  region  are stored in  modkit 188 

pileup BED files  and  variant VCF files within the same directory, enabling fine -grained 189 

analysis of TE loci of interest. 190 

In p arallel , non -ref -TE insertions are identified  and classified  from the ONT reads  using 191 

the widely adopted  long -read TE detection  tool  TLDR  (Ewing  et al. , 2020) . TLDR  outputs a 192 

single  summary file  listing  all detected insertions  and , for each  individual  TE insertion, a 193 

locally assemble d TE consensus sequence  (FASTA format ) and  a corresponding 194 

alignment  file ( BAM format) . Based on these  outputs , ECHO implements  a custom  bash 195 

script  to  filter for high -quality insertions (filter = PASS) and  uses modkit to subsequently 196 

calculate  single -CpG  and regional  methylation levels  per  insertion  and  its flanking 197 

regions . The resulting  ECHO  output  extends the TLDR summary with  regional  methylation 198 

metrics  for each non -ref -TE  (phased/unphased) , complementing the reference -based  TE 199

## 3 Installation and usage 202 

ECHO  is distributed  and maintained  via a public GitHub repository, which provides 203 

detailed installation instructions, documentation , and test data.  The workflow requires 204 

Snakemake and Singularity to be available on the target system.  Snakemake p ipeline 205 

configuration is defined through a YAML file specifying sample lists, reference genome, 206 

TR and TE catalogues, input data formats, and additional analysis parameters. 207 

Configuration files  can be  generated using a dedicated Python script , which  also 208 

performs consistency checks and validation prior to execution.  Bundled  ECHO  repeat 209 

catalogue s are hosted on  Zenodo and can be  downloaded  upon installation  to ensure 210 

consistency with the pipeline  setup (see Data  Availability and Github) . Pipeline execution 211 

is controlled via  run  profiles supporting both  HPC  environments  (recommended)  and 212 

local executions . These profiles  define  the  pipeline config file , and system -dependent 213 

settings , such as  job scheduler  (e.g. SLURM or PBS)  and  resource allocation , which  can 214 

be adapted to the  target  infrastructure.  After configuration, ECHO can be  run using  a215 

single Snakemake command specifying  the relevant  execution profile.  To facilitate 216 

reproducible execution and user validation, ECHO is accompanied by  two test ONT 217 

datasets: a lightweight smoke -test dataset and a whole -genome dataset ( HG002  15× ). 218 

Both datasets , together with  corresponding expected outputs are hosted on Zenodo (see 219 

Data  Availability). 220 

## 4 Performance evaluation 221 

To  benchmark  pipeline  performance , we  applied  ECHO  to publicly available  Genome in a 222 

Bottle ( GIAB ) HG002  ONT data  (see Data  Availability ), basecalled  on  a PromethION 24 223 

compute tower  using  Dorado v0.8.3  with the  sup_v5,5mCG_5hmCG  model.  Reads were 224 

sub sequently sub sampled  to  approximately  30×  and 15 × genomic  coverage  to evaluate 225 

performance across different sequencing depths.  ECHO was  run on a HPC system using 226 

default  configuration  setting s with the  GRCh38 reference build and bundled repeat 227 

catalogues ( Supplementary  Figure  1). 228

## 5 Conclusion 258 

We have developed ECHO, a portable , reproducible , and  scalable  Snakemake  pipeline 259 

for comprehensive (epi)genomic characterization of  the human repeatome  using  ONT 260 

sequencing . By harmonising existing alignment, variant calling, phasing, repeat 261 

genotyping and methylation analysis tools within a single workflow, ECHO enables 262 

integrated and  haplotype -resolved  analysis of (epi)genetic variation across  multiple 263 

classes of  repetitive DNA  elements, which  traditionally  have been analysed using 264 

separate, specialized approaches  so far . Its  modular design  allows  straightforward 265 

adjustment, extension , and integration of additional analysis  components , ensuring  long -266 

term  flexibility  and compatibility with future tools and updates.  Overall, we expect that 267 

ECHO will enable and accelerate human repeatome research in the near future at both 268 

individual and population level. 269
